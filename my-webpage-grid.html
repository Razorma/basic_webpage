Barberton Museum

  • The Barberton Museum is located in the historic town of Barberton, which is situated in the Mpumalanga province of South Africa. The town is known for its rich history and is considered to be the oldest in the province.
  • The museum was established in 1884 and is one of the oldest museums in South Africa. It was initially created as a place to house collections of plants and animals from the region, but over time it expanded to include collections of art, history, and geology.
  • One of the main attractions at the Barberton Museum is the collection of fossils from the Barberton Greenstone Belt. This area is considered to be one of the oldest and most well-preserved rock formations in the world, and the fossils found here are some of the oldest on record. Visitors can see a variety of fossils, including those of early bacteria and single-celled organisms, as well as evidence of ancient volcanic activity.

pilgrims rest

  • Pilgrim's Rest is a small town in the Mpumalanga province of South Africa that was founded during the 1870s gold rush.
  • The town was declared a National Monument in 1986 and has been preserved as a living museum, showcasing what life was like in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.
  • Visitors to Pilgrim's Rest can explore the town's historic buildings, including the Royal Hotel, which was built in 1894 and is still in operation today, and the Alanglade House Museum, which offers a glimpse into the lives of wealthy mine owners during the town's heyday.
